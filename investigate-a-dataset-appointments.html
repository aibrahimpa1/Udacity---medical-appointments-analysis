Introduction¶

This dataset collects informationfrom 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patients are included in the data and we will try to find out which factors affect the outcome of a scheduled appointment, i.e if the patient will show up or not.

Conclusion¶

The data is imbalanced. The only variables correlated to No-show column (patient showing up for the appointment or skipping it) were SMS_received and Appointment_ID. Approximately the same ratio of male and female patients shows up for their appointments. When it comes to life period, we can conclude that young people tend to miss their appointments the most, then mid-life, and old people are the most responsible, given from ratio of showing up and not showing up for all three categories. I have to accentuate that the difference is not significant, only noticable. We can also tell that most of the appointments is in the morning, and the least in the evening (which was expected). When it comes to diseases, we can conclude from the heatmap that Age is directly correlated with Diabetes and Hypetension.
